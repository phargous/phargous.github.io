About Me

A bit about me

My whole childhood, I have lived close to the sea. For sure it has oriented my choices to be today a PhD student in physical oceanography. I graduated from ENSEEIHT (a french engineering school) as an engineer in fluid mechaninics and obtained a Msc in Ocean, Atmosphere and Climate Sciences. During my career, I moved one year to the Canary Islands where I had the opportunity to work for the first time in an investigation team at IOCAG (Instituto de Oceanografía y Cambio Global) and in a company (Elittoral). Increasingly curious about physical oceanography I pursued with my master thesis in Tasmania at CSIRO (Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation). After this journey, I was even more curious about the physical processes in the ocean and decided to start a PhD at IMEDEA (Mediterranean Institute for Advanced Studies).

About my research

PhD candidate in physical oceanography. I work on ocean mesoscale variability in the Mediterranean Sea particularly I focused on eddy kinetic energy (EKE) trends and variability during the altimetric era. I compared different gridded satellites products (geostrophic currents, eddy atlases) and tested the robustness of the trends with along-track data. My expertise includes analyzing large oceanographic in-situ, satellite and model data sets using MATLAB and Python. I also have experience working with Linux in bash and high-performance computing environments.
