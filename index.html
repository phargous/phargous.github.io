A bit about me

My whole childhood, I have lived close to the sea. For sure it has oriented my choices to be today a PhD student in physical oceanography. I double graduated from a french engineering school (ENSEEIHT) and a Msc in Ocean, Atmosphere and Climate Sciences. During my career I had the opportunity to spend one year in the Canary Islands where I did two six months internships at IOCAG (Instituto de Oceanografía y Cambio Global) and Elittoral. I then did my master practice in CSIRO (Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation) before starting a PhD at IMEDEA (Instituto Mediterraneo de Estudios Avanzados) where I am studying the mesoscale activity.

About my research

PhD candidate in physical oceanography. I work on ocean mesoscale variability in the Mediterranean Sea. I compared different gridded satellite products (geostrophic velocities, eddy atlases) to study Eddy Kinetic Energy trends in the Mediterranean Sea and assess the robustness of these trends using along-track satellite data. The next step is studying the fine-scales dynamics of specific regions using wide-swath altimetry (SWOT).
